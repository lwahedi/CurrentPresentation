Meso: Distributed Governance

Who Are We?

Meso is a distributed platform that draws on network theory and machine learning to facilitate problem solving through knowledge transfer. The platform integrates top-down and grassroots priorities to enable local solutions to global problems.

Founders

Laila A. Wahedi, PhD

Wahedi is a political scientist/data scientist at the Massive Data Institute in the McCourt School of Public Policy at Georgetown University. Wahedi studies the network dynamics of how organizations and individuals interact. She has worked with the US Defense Department on strategic force planning, and was a US delegate for the Bosch Global Governance Fellowship.

Anna Waldman-Brown

Waldman-Brown is a researcher at Legatum Center for Development and Entrepreneurship at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where she investigates strategies to mitigate technology-driven inequality. A Fulbright fellow, she also works with the Fab Lab network and makerspaces across 60+ countries to foster sustainable development.

Salman Aldukheil

Aldukheil is a social entrepreneur and financial analyst from Riyadh. He has worked with a startup incubator in Berlin and a VC firm in Chicago, and is a former consultant at McKinsey & Company where he developed strategies for inclusive economic development in Saudi Arabia.
